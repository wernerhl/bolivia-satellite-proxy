\begin{abstract}
\noindent
This paper constructs the first monthly coincident indicator of Bolivian real activity from four independent satellite streams --- VIIRS Day/Night Band nighttime radiance over eleven urban buffers, VIIRS Nightfire radiant heat over the six major Chaco gas fields, Sentinel-5P TROPOMI tropospheric nitrogen-dioxide columns over the La Paz--El Alto and Santa Cruz metropolitan areas, and Sentinel-2 NDVI over five Bolivian cropland zones --- covering approximately \num{78} percent of GDP. The four streams are combined through a two-factor dynamic factor model separating urban and extractive activity, with an auxiliary agricultural factor conditioning on NDVI. % BEGIN-AUTO-headline Headline: the satellite factor bottoms at $z=-2.62$ in 2024-01; latest (2026-04) at $z=-0.98$.  % END-AUTO-headline The indicator arrives at monthly frequency with a two-month publication lag, compared to a two- to three-month lag on quarterly GDP and a March 2028 expected date for twenty-four vintages of INE's new experimental IGAE. I apply the indicator to Bolivia's \numrange{2025}{2026} recession and demonstrate three manipulation-detection tests built around single-country sources of identification: sectoral triangulation across VIIRS Nightfire, YPFB-reported gas production, and INE hydrocarbon value added; a November 2025 INE-leadership discontinuity regression; and external-forecaster residual falsification.
\vspace{0.5em}

\noindent \textbf{JEL:} C32, C38, E01, E32, O11, O54, Q15, Q35, Q43. \\
\noindent \textbf{Keywords:} nighttime lights, VIIRS, TROPOMI, NDVI, dynamic factor model, nowcasting, coincident indicator, GDP manipulation, Bolivia, natural gas, recession dating.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
% ============================================================

\claim{Bolivia entered a recession of contested depth in 2025} The national statistics office (INE) reported a \num{-1.58} percent contraction for the full year, a number released on \num{21} April 2026 with the revision of the national accounts to a 2017 base year. The International Monetary Fund projects \num{-3.3} percent for 2026 against INE's own implicit forecast of a return to growth; the World Bank forecasts \num{-3.2} percent, Oxford Economics \num{-0.7} percent, and Standard \& Poor's \num{-1.3} percent. The spread across external forecasters is itself a measurement problem --- but the interesting measurement problem is the spread between INE and any of them.

\claim{The independence of Bolivia's statistical production is a first-order concern} INE's director is appointed by the executive. The \numrange{2014}{2023} commodity-cycle decade combined fiscal deficits above \num{10} percent of GDP, international reserves falling from \$\num{14} billion to \$\num{103} million, and a currency peg held nominally constant at \num{6.96} bolivianos per dollar while a parallel rate drifted to two and then three multiples of the official. Inflation in the official Consumer Price Index remained in single digits during 2023--2024 even as imported-goods shortages and fuel queues were binding. The Fund in its May 2025 Article~IV consultation called the policy mix ``unsustainable''; the ratings agencies downgraded sovereign debt to CCC$-$; the Fernald--Hsu--Spiegel (2021) manipulation prior, the Michalski--Stoltz (2013) fixed-regime-with-reserve-collapse prior, and the \citet{martinez2022} autocracy-residual prior all point the same way.

\claim{Satellite observations of the Bolivian economy do not depend on INE} I build four monthly activity proxies, each sensitive to a different slice of real activity, each measured independently by an instrument that Bolivia does not operate and cannot touch. Nighttime radiance from the Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite (VIIRS) Day/Night Band over eleven Bolivian urban buffers proxies urban formal-sector activity and commerce, after Gibson et al. (2021) established that VIIRS dominates the older Defense Meteorological Satellite Program (DMSP) data at low and medium density. Radiant heat from VIIRS Nightfire (VNF) over the six major Chaco gas fields --- Margarita--Huacaya, San Alberto, Sábalo, Incahuasi, Aquio, and Itaú --- proxies upstream hydrocarbon activity after \citet{elvidge_vnf2013} and the \citet{do_etal2018} demonstration that VNF can invert radiant heat into volumetric measures under specific gas-chemistry conditions. Tropospheric nitrogen-dioxide columns from the TROPOspheric Monitoring Instrument (TROPOMI) on Sentinel-5P over La Paz--El Alto and Santa Cruz proxy the traffic and combustion signal of two metros that jointly account for approximately \num{40} percent of national value added (La Paz department \num{25} percent plus Santa Cruz department \num{31} percent, net of the periurban and rural components; INE 2024 departmental GDP). NDVI from Sentinel-2 over five cropland zones proxies agricultural activity, the single Bolivian sector expanding in 2025.

\claim{The four streams triangulate four dimensions of the recession} Nighttime lights are the established activity proxy of \citet{hsw2012}; they do not distinguish extractive from urban activity, and they do not see agriculture. Gas flares isolate the hydrocarbon channel, which matters unusually much in Bolivia because tax and foreign-exchange revenues concentrate there. Tropospheric NO$_2$ isolates the urban-combustion channel, which responds within weeks to mobility and industrial shocks and which, post-fuel-subsidy elimination in December 2025, is no longer contaminated by the smuggling arbitrage that made Bolivian fuel-sales data uninformative for a decade. NDVI isolates the agricultural channel, which is invisible to the first three. Jointly the four streams cover approximately \num{78} percent of GDP, leaving mainly non-tradeable services as the explicit blind spot.

\claim{This paper makes two contributions} First, I build the first published satellite triangulation for a low-capacity statistical environment, combining four independent streams (VIIRS DNB, VIIRS Nightfire, Sentinel-5P NO${}_2$, and Sentinel-2 NDVI) into a monthly coincident indicator of goods-producing and tradeable-services activity covering approximately \num{78} percent of GDP. \citet{bolivar_cuba2024} nowcast INE's own quarterly GDP using machine-learning methods on INE inputs; this paper's design is distinct --- it cross-checks INE from outside rather than forecasts it from within. Second, I apply the indicator to Bolivia's \numrange{2025}{2026} recession, document the sectoral composition the national accounts cannot yet deliver at monthly frequency, and test three manipulation hypotheses built around a single-country discontinuity at the November 2025 INE leadership change, external-forecaster residuals, and VIIRS Nightfire--YPFB--INE sectoral triangulation.

\claim{The remainder of the paper is organized as follows} Section~\ref{sec:context} documents Bolivia's \numrange{2014}{2026} macroeconomic deterioration and the institutional constraints on INE. Section~\ref{sec:data} describes the four satellite data streams, the spatial definitions, and the official series used for cross-validation. Section~\ref{sec:empirical} specifies the empirical framework --- single-series elasticities, the two-factor mixed-frequency DFM, and the three-test manipulation-detection ladder. Section~\ref{sec:results} \tbdline{reports the results}. Section~\ref{sec:robustness} \tbdline{reports robustness exercises}. Section~\ref{sec:conclusion} concludes.

\section{Institutional and macroeconomic context}
\label{sec:context}
% ============================================================

\claim{Bolivia's \numrange{2014}{2026} macroeconomic trajectory has two acts} Act one is a decade of falling natural-gas revenues masked by reserve depletion, monetary financing of the treasury, and a currency peg held nominally constant. Between the \num{2014} commodity peak and the end of \num{2024}, international reserves fell from \$\num{14.0} billion to under \$\num{2} billion; by September \num{2025}, usable liquid reserves were \$\num{103} million, against a monthly import bill of roughly \$\num{1.2} billion. Act two is the \num{2025} political rupture: the November \num{2025} inauguration of Rodrigo Paz ended two decades of \emph{Movimiento al Socialismo} (MAS) rule, fuel subsidies were eliminated in December, multilateral financing of more than \$\num{8} billion was secured, and the sovereign risk spread compressed from \num{2002} basis points in February \num{2025} to \num{466} by mid-February \num{2026}. INE's \numrange{2025}{2026} statistical production spans both acts and was edited by two different administrations.

\claim{The hydrocarbons collapse is the mechanical core of the recession} Natural-gas production peaked at roughly \num{60} million cubic metres per day in \num{2014} and fell to approximately \num{30} by end-\num{2024}; the fourth quarter of \num{2025} registered a year-on-year decline of \num{10.51} percent in oil-and-gas extraction according to INE. The six fields in the Chaco basin --- Margarita--Huacaya, San Alberto, Sábalo, Incahuasi, Aquio, and Itaú --- account for roughly \num{90} percent of national output. Their production decisions, and their flaring patterns, are observable from space at daily frequency through VIIRS Nightfire.

\claim{Construction is the second-largest drag on activity} INE reports construction output down \num{13.57} percent for full-year \num{2025} and \num{-17.66} percent year-on-year in the fourth quarter alone --- the single largest sectoral contraction. Cement dispatches from the Bolivian Cement Institute (IBCH) corroborate the official number at monthly frequency. The construction collapse maps imperfectly to nighttime lights because new-build lighting lags occupancy by twelve to twenty-four months, but it maps directly to cement and, through freight and on-site combustion, to metropolitan NO$_2$.

\claim{Commerce and services have contracted more modestly, agriculture has expanded} The fourth-quarter interannual prints are \num{-6.05} percent for commerce and \num{+12.19} percent for agriculture, yielding the unusual pattern of a Latin American recession with an expanding primary sector. Agriculture is essentially invisible to NTL, largely invisible to VNF, and invisible to metropolitan TROPOMI; the satellite composite therefore under-measures the true level of activity but --- critically --- its \emph{direction} and \emph{turning points} remain informative. The point is developed in Section~\ref{sec:empirical}.

\claim{The official coincident indicator is new and short} INE began publishing an experimental monthly Índice Global de Actividad Económica (IGAE) with a \num{2017} base year in March \num{2026}, under IMF technical assistance. The new series will not have twenty-four vintages until March \num{2028}. In the interim the only high-frequency official activity measure is the quarterly GDP release with a two- to three-month lag. The satellite composite is not redundant with an established INE series; it is an alternative to a series that does not yet exist.

\claim{The BCB's monetary execution is the closest thing to a transparent activity signal} The Central Bank of Bolivia's \emph{Seguimiento al Programa Monetario} provides monthly data on credit from the BCB to the Treasury (CIN-SPNF), open-market operations, and international reserves. January \num{2026} execution showed a sharp contractionary break: CIN-SPNF at $-$Bs~\num{1551} million against a programmed $+$Bs~\num{42} million, reflecting Treasury net repayment rather than monetization. The April \num{2026} \emph{Minuta de Política Monetaria} states that ``la pasada gestión habría sido el punto más bajo de la actividad económica'' --- the Bank's call that Q4 \num{2025} was the trough. The satellite composite adjudicates.

\subsubsection{VIIRS Day/Night Band nighttime radiance}

\claim{I use the monthly VIIRS DNB composite} The primary source is the NASA Black Marble product \texttt{VNP46A3}, monthly at \num{500}~m resolution, BRDF-corrected, snow-free, and gap-filled. The fallback is the Earth Observation Group (EOG) cloud-free monthly composite \texttt{DNB/MONTHLY\_V1}. Radiance values below \num{0.5}~nW$\cdot$cm$^{-2}\cdot$sr$^{-1}$ are masked as background; values above \num{200}~nW$\cdot$cm$^{-2}\cdot$sr$^{-1}$ are masked as aberration. VIIRS is 14-bit and does not saturate at Bolivian urban densities; DMSP is 6-bit and saturates in Santa Cruz and La Paz--El Alto, which is why DMSP is not used. The \citet{gibson_etal2021} assessment is dispositive on that choice.

\claim{The city buffers are circular, centered on the urban core, with radii scaled to metro footprint} Eleven buffers span the nine departmental capitals plus El Alto (merged with La Paz) and Montero, Yacuiba, and Cobija. The consolidated spatial definitions for all four streams appear in Table~\ref{tab:spatial}. Sum of lights (SOL) is the buffer-integrated radiance in each month; I also report mean and median radiance, the count of valid pixels, and the count of masked pixels.

\input{tables/table1_spatial.tex}

\claim{The baseline period is January \num{2013} through December \num{2019}} The choice predates the COVID shock and the \num{2023} parallel-exchange-rate emergence, providing seven years of pre-crisis monthly observations. Deseasonalization uses robust STL with a monthly period; the anomaly is the log deviation from the baseline trend extrapolated linearly. Cloud contamination matters in the Santa Cruz wet season (November--March); months with valid-pixel counts below \num{50} percent of the buffer total are flagged and, when required by the DFM step, imputed via the Kalman smoother on the joint state.

\subsubsection{VIIRS Nightfire radiant heat}

\claim{Gas flares are sub-pixel hot sources, not ordinary surface radiance} The VIIRS Nightfire algorithm of \citet{elvidge_vnf2013} fits a Planck curve to multispectral observations and recovers source temperature, effective area, and radiant heat output (RH, in MW) per detection, using the mid-infrared and near-infrared VIIRS bands that resolve temperatures above \num{1400}~K. Gas flares cluster between \num{1600} and \num{2000}~K; biomass combustion peaks between \num{800} and \num{1200}~K and is excluded by a temperature floor of \num{1400}~K. I pull nightly VNF detections from the EOG archive for the Chaco bounding box $[-22.5, -20.0]$ latitude $\times$ $[-64.5, -62.5]$ longitude and assign each detection to the nearest of the six major fields within a \num{2}~km radius; unassigned detections are discarded. The six fields and their operators appear in Table~\ref{tab:spatial}.

\claim{VNF detections are noisy at the daily level and clean at the monthly level} The monthly field-level signal is the sum of per-detection radiant heat, $\textstyle \sum_t \text{RH}_t$, measured in MW-nights. Daily detections are sparse (one to five per field per night, varying with cloud cover and orbit geometry), but monthly aggregation typically yields fifty to two hundred detections per field and a signal-to-noise ratio adequate to resolve production changes on the order of ten percent. The \citet{elvidge_energies2016} calibration converts MW-nights into billion cubic metres (BCM) of flared gas, which maps to produced gas through field-specific flare-to-production ratios estimated on the pre-crisis sample.

\subsubsection{Sentinel-5P TROPOMI tropospheric NO$_2$}

\claim{TROPOMI resolves the Bolivian metros} The TROPOspheric Monitoring Instrument on the European Space Agency's Sentinel-5P satellite, described in \citet{veefkind2012}, provides daily global coverage at \num{5.5}$\times$\num{3.5}~km resolution (post-August \num{2019} upgrade) with a \num{13}:\num{30} local solar overpass. I use the offline-reprocessed Level~3 product \texttt{COPERNICUS/S5P/OFFL/L3\_NO2} with quality filter \texttt{qa\_value $\geq$ 0.75}. The tropospheric NO$_2$ vertical column density, in \unit{mol\per m\squared}, is the relevant retrieval; stratospheric NO$_2$ is subtracted in the Level~3 processing. The bounding rectangles for La Paz--El Alto, Santa Cruz, and Cochabamba appear in Table~\ref{tab:spatial}.

\claim{The TROPOMI series is short and the baseline is tight} The sensor became operational in July \num{2018}. The baseline period is calendar year \num{2019} --- the only full pre-COVID, pre-Bolivia-crisis year available --- which implies that month-of-year seasonal factors are point estimates with no standard error until additional clean years accumulate. I therefore weight TROPOMI at \num{0.20} in the composite rather than \num{0.30}, with the weights revisited annually. Cloud-contaminated months (fewer than fifteen valid days) are flagged and imputed through the DFM. Andean volcanic NO$_2$ from Sabancaya and Ubinas on the Peruvian side can contaminate La Paz retrievals and is flagged when monthly values exceed five times the baseline.

\subsubsection{Sentinel-2 NDVI over Bolivian cropland}

\claim{Agriculture expanded \num{12.19} percent year-on-year in Q4 \num{2025} and is invisible to the first three streams} An activity composite that misses the one expanding sector cannot claim to be a coincident indicator of real activity; it is a coincident indicator of contracting activity only. I close the gap with Sentinel-2 NDVI over five Bolivian cropland zones. Normalized Difference Vegetation Index, $\mathrm{NDVI} = (B_8 - B_4) / (B_8 + B_4)$, is the standard satellite vegetation-activity proxy of \citet{gao1996} and is widely used as a crop-yield forecaster since \citet{johnson_etal2014} and as a GPP proxy since \citet{donohue_etal2008}; \citet{donaldson_storeygard2016} review its use in economics.

\claim{Five zones cover more than \num{90} percent of Bolivian agricultural value added} The zones appear in Table~\ref{tab:spatial}, with departmental agricultural-GVA weights from the INE \num{2024} national accounts. I intersect each zone with the ESA WorldCover \num{2021} cropland mask (class \num{40}) and compute zone-monthly mean NDVI over the crop-masked pixels only, so non-crop land cover does not contaminate the signal.

\claim{I splice Landsat-8 onto Sentinel-2 to recover the 2013--2016 baseline} Sentinel-2 Level-2A \texttt{COPERNICUS/S2\_SR\_HARMONIZED} is operational from March \num{2017}; I extend the series to January \num{2013} using Landsat-8 Collection 2 Level-2 surface reflectance \texttt{LANDSAT/LC08/C02/T1\_L2} with the \citet{roy_etal2016} cross-sensor NDVI harmonization coefficients ($\mathrm{NDVI}_{S2} = 1.00 \cdot \mathrm{NDVI}_{L8} + 0.01$). The deseasonalized anomaly is $(\mathrm{NDVI}_{zt} - \mu_{z,m}) / \sigma_z$, where $\mu_{z,m}$ is the zone-by-month-of-year mean on \numrange{2013}{2019} and $\sigma_z$ is the pooled-across-baseline standard deviation. Zones are weighted by departmental agricultural-GVA shares to form the agricultural composite.

\subsubsection{Sectoral coverage and the remaining blind spot}

\claim{The four streams cover approximately \num{78} percent of GDP} VIIRS DNB and TROPOMI NO$_2$ together proxy urban formal-sector activity and combustion (\num{55} percent of GDP). VNF proxies hydrocarbon extraction (\num{20} percent). NDVI proxies agriculture and related primary activity (\num{14} percent, net of small overlaps). The remaining \num{11} percent is largely non-tradeable services: health, education, public administration, domestic work. This is the paper's explicit blind spot, discussed in Section~\ref{sec:conclusion}.

\subsection{Official series for cross-validation}

\claim{Five INE, BCB, and sectoral series provide the ground truth to be tested} Quarterly GDP from INE, at \num{2017} constant prices; monthly IGAE from INE, from March \num{2026}; monthly cement dispatches from IBCH; monthly electricity generation from the Comité Nacional de Despacho de Carga (CNDC); monthly natural-gas production from YPFB; monthly IVA and IT tax collections from the Servicio de Impuestos Nacionales (SIN), deflated to \num{2018} base by INE's CPI; and monthly merchandise imports by volume and value from Aduana Nacional. Monetary execution --- CIN-SPNF, OMAs, reserves --- comes from the BCB's monthly \emph{Seguimiento al Programa Monetario}. The parallel exchange-rate premium comes from my own Bolivia Early Warning System dashboard, which aggregates Binance P2P USDT/BOB quotes at daily frequency.

\claim{Ground truth is a contested object in this setting} Much of this section reports series that are themselves under suspicion. The strategy is not to benchmark satellite measurements against official numbers assumed correct --- that would defeat the purpose --- but to locate the dimensions along which satellite and official measures covary, the dimensions along which they diverge, and the economic content of those divergences.

\section{Empirical framework}
\label{sec:empirical}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Single-series elasticities}

\claim{The VIIRS-to-GDP elasticity is the anchor of the nighttime-lights literature} \citet{hsw2012} report cross-country elasticities near \num{0.3} annually; \citet{beyer_hu_yao2022} report larger elasticities (\numrange{1.36}{1.81}) at quarterly frequency for emerging-market and developing economies, reflecting higher noise-to-signal in monthly VIIRS composites and the correction for the effective-observation issue. I estimate the Bolivia-specific elasticity on departmental GDP for the seven departments with continuous VIIRS coverage over \numrange{2013}{2024}, using the specification

\begin{equation}
\Delta \log(\text{GDP}_{d,t}) = \alpha_d + \beta \cdot \Delta \log(\text{SOL}_{d,t}) + \gamma_t + \varepsilon_{d,t},
\label{eq:viirs_elasticity}
\end{equation}

where $d$ indexes department, $t$ indexes year, $\alpha_d$ is a department fixed effect, and $\gamma_t$ is a year fixed effect absorbing common macroeconomic shocks. Standard errors are two-way clustered by department and year. The identifying assumption is that measurement error in VIIRS SOL is orthogonal to measurement error in INE departmental GDP --- not a classical assumption, but plausible given that the two are produced by wholly unrelated production systems.

\claim{The VNF-to-gas-production elasticity is the anchor for the hydrocarbon stream} The specification parallels equation~\eqref{eq:viirs_elasticity}:

\begin{equation}
\Delta \log(\text{GasProd}_{f,t}) = \alpha_f + \beta_{\text{VNF}} \cdot \Delta \log(\text{RH}_{f,t}) + \gamma_t + \varepsilon_{f,t},
\label{eq:vnf_elasticity}
\end{equation}

where $f$ indexes field, $\text{GasProd}$ is YPFB-reported monthly production, and $\text{RH}$ is the monthly sum of VNF radiant heat for the field's \num{2}~km buffer. \citet{do_etal2018} found an elasticity near unity for ISIS-controlled oil fields, validated against captured documents; Bolivian dry-gas chemistry differs, which is why I estimate Bolivia-specific field elasticities rather than importing the ISIS coefficient. The pre-crisis sample --- \numrange{2012}{2022}, before YPFB reporting came under credibility pressure --- is the calibration window.

\claim{The TROPOMI-to-activity elasticity requires a specification that accommodates the fuel-subsidy break} The December \num{2025} fuel-subsidy elimination shifted the mapping from real-world fuel consumption to measured NO$_2$ in both directions: lower smuggling-related consumption (which had been unmeasured) and higher domestic prices (which suppress measured consumption). I estimate

\begin{equation}
\Delta \log(\text{NO}_{2,m,t}) = \alpha_m + \beta_{\text{NO}_2} \cdot \Delta \log(\text{FuelSales}_{m,t}) + \delta \cdot D^{\text{post-Dec25}}_t + \gamma_t + \varepsilon_{m,t},
\label{eq:no2_elasticity}
\end{equation}

where $m$ indexes metro, $\text{FuelSales}$ is YPFB distribution data for the metro area, $D^{\text{post-Dec25}}$ is a post-break indicator, and the specification is run separately for the La Paz--El Alto, Santa Cruz, and Cochabamba ROIs. \citet{bauwens2020} and \citet{liu_china2020} document the COVID-era TROPOMI--activity relationship in Europe and China; the Bolivian coefficient must be estimated locally because urban-fleet composition, altitude (La Paz sits at \num{3650}~m), and thermal-inversion patterns differ.

\subsection{Mixed-frequency dynamic factor model}

\claim{The three streams are combined into a common activity factor} I use a mixed-frequency DFM following \citet{mariano_murasawa2003} and \citet{grs2008}, with the state-space representation

\begin{align}
y_{i,t} &= \lambda_i \cdot f_t + e_{i,t}, \qquad i = 1, \ldots, N \label{eq:dfm_obs}\\
f_t &= \phi_1 f_{t-1} + \phi_2 f_{t-2} + \eta_t, \qquad \eta_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma_\eta^2) \label{eq:dfm_state}\\
e_{i,t} &= \rho_i e_{i,t-1} + \varepsilon_{i,t}, \qquad \varepsilon_{i,t} \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma_i^2) \label{eq:dfm_idio}
\end{align}

where $y_{i,t}$ are standardized monthly anomalies of the three satellite streams plus cement dispatches, electricity generation, real tax collections, and import volumes (for a total of $N = 7$ indicators); $f_t$ is the latent monthly activity factor; $\lambda_i$ is the factor loading; and $e_{i,t}$ is idiosyncratic noise following an AR(1) process. Quarterly GDP enters through the \citet{mariano_murasawa2003} aggregation identity

\begin{equation}
\Delta \log \text{GDP}^Q_t = \frac{1}{3}\left(f_t + 2 f_{t-1} + 3 f_{t-2} + 2 f_{t-3} + f_{t-4}\right) + \xi_t,
\label{eq:mm_aggregation}
\end{equation}

which approximates the three-month geometric-mean aggregation of unobserved monthly GDP under log-linearization. The model is estimated by the EM algorithm with Kalman smoothing.

\claim{News-decomposition of the monthly index follows Banbura--Giannone--Modugno--Reichlin} As each new satellite observation or official release arrives, the change in the filtered $\hat{f}_t$ can be attributed to specific indicators using the decomposition in \citet{bgmr2013}. This generates a running attribution table reporting how much of each month's activity change is due to VIIRS, to VNF, to NO$_2$, and to each of the four official indicators, with standard errors. The attribution table is the primary operational output of the pipeline for the La Linterna newsroom.

\claim{Recession dating uses BBQ and Markov-switching} The \citet{harding_pagan2002} BBQ algorithm applied to $\hat{f}_t$ yields deterministic peak and trough dates; the \citet{hamilton1989} two-state Markov-switching AR on $\hat{f}_t$ yields monthly recession probabilities. The two approaches are complementary: BBQ gives a clean dating for retrospective analysis and Markov-switching gives a real-time probability for nowcasting.

\subsection{Manipulation-detection test}

\claim{\citet{martinez2022} identifies off 184 countries of regime variation} Bolivia supplies one. The cross-country identification is unavailable; the test must be redesigned around single-country sources of identification. I implement three tests, ordered as a ladder of evidence: the physical layer, the statistical layer, and the consensus layer. A rejection at all three is the strong claim; a rejection at the physical layer alone is the weak-but-publishable claim.

\claim{Test 1 (physical layer) --- sectoral triangulation across VNF, YPFB, and INE} VIIRS Nightfire measures a physical, tamper-evident observation: the radiant heat emitted by the Chaco flares. YPFB reports gas production. INE reports hydrocarbon value added. In a no-manipulation world the three series must track each other up to operational noise. I estimate the log--log correlation of $\Sigma\,\mathrm{RH}$ against reported gas production and of gas production against INE hydrocarbon value added on the pre-crisis window (\numrange{2012}{2023}) and then project both regressions onto the 2024--2026 window. A divergence at the first step (VNF--YPFB) localizes manipulation to the source-data level; a divergence at the second step (YPFB--INE) but not the first localizes it to the national-accounts aggregation. Identifying assumption: flaring is a physical observation that no Bolivian institution controls.

\claim{Test 2 (statistical layer) --- November 2025 INE leadership discontinuity} INE's director changed on November 8, 2025 with the Paz inauguration. Hydrocarbon fundamentals, reserves trajectory, and the parallel-rate premium evolve continuously across the break. I estimate
\begin{equation}
\Delta \log(\text{IGAE}_t) = \alpha + \beta \cdot \Delta \log(\text{CI}_t) + \delta \cdot D^{\text{post-Nov25}}_t + \gamma \cdot [\Delta \log(\text{CI}_t) \cdot D^{\text{post-Nov25}}_t] + X_t \cdot \psi + \varepsilon_t,
\label{eq:leadership_break}
\end{equation}
where $\text{CI}_t$ is the satellite composite, $D^{\text{post-Nov25}}$ is the leadership-change indicator, and $X_t$ is a control vector containing the parallel premium, the reserve change, month dummies, and precipitation. The null is $\gamma = 0$; the alternative $\gamma > 0$ is the Martinez signature (pre-Nov INE absorbed less satellite-implied activity movement per unit of real signal). Bolivia's monthly IGAE starts March 2026, so the post-break sample has roughly five observations at the submission vintage; I flag the test as preliminary and re-estimate at R\&R. Where IGAE is too short, I substitute quarterly GDP with the \citet{mariano_murasawa2003} aggregation. Identifying assumption: the satellite composite is continuous across the leadership change because the satellites do not care who runs INE.

\claim{Test 3 (consensus layer) --- external-forecaster residual} I regress the annualized satellite composite separately on (i) INE reported growth and (ii) the mean of IMF, World Bank, Oxford Economics, and Standard \& Poor's growth forecasts for \num{2025} and \num{2026}. The residuals against external consensus and against INE are of different sign during the 2024--2025 acute crisis if INE smoothed upward while external forecasters did not. The null is equal residual magnitudes; rejection in favor of a smaller satellite residual against external consensus supports the INE-smoothing interpretation. Identifying assumption: external forecasters draw on approximately the same macro-data universe as the satellite composite (reserves, prices, trade flows) and produce numbers independent of INE; their residuals against the satellite are therefore a benchmark for what a consensus reading of the recession would look like.

\claim{The ladder interpretation} A positive finding is not a claim of fraud. It is a claim that the INE aggregates, whether by construction or by design, understate the \numrange{2025}{2026} contraction by a magnitude the satellite evidence can quantify. A rejection at the physical layer alone, without the statistical or consensus layers, is the weakest publishable claim and the closest to what the current vintage supports; a rejection at all three is the strongest claim and requires either more post-break IGAE data or stronger sectoral-triangulation divergence.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
% ============================================================

\claim{A four-stream satellite coincident indicator for Bolivia} The indicator covers approximately \num{78} percent of GDP at monthly frequency with a two-month publication lag, arriving two to three months before the quarterly national accounts and meeting the full twenty-four-vintage threshold of INE's experimental IGAE only in March \num{2028}. \tbdline{Magnitude and timing of the satellite-implied recession vs.\ INE.}

\claim{The remaining blind spot is non-tradeable services} Health, education, public administration, and domestic work together account for approximately \num{11} percent of GDP and are not visible to any of the four streams. A satellite composite that excludes them is a coincident indicator of \emph{goods-producing and tradeable-services} activity, not of GDP in full. This caveat carries through every numerical comparison with INE; the magnitude of the satellite--INE gap is partly a feature of measurement coverage, not of manipulation alone.

\claim{Three directions for extension} (i) Extend the manipulation-detection framework to CPI using online-scraped prices in the \citet{cavallo2013} tradition. (ii) Pair the satellite composite with a high-frequency fiscal-dominance indicator combining BCB's CIN-SPNF with the parallel-rate premium. (iii) Apply the framework to the \numrange{2026}{2027} Paz reform program in real time: the satellite indicator is well suited to testing whether stabilization is being captured in the national accounts or understated. The framework applies directly to Venezuela, Angola, and post-\num{2021} Afghanistan.

\claim{The satellite data are free and the framework is open-source} Replication materials, processing pipeline, and live dashboard at \url{wernerhl.github.io/bolivia-ews}. The frozen replication dataset is archived at Zenodo DOI \tbdline{10.5281/zenodo.XXXXXX}.
